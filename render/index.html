Welcome to www.SHRDLU.

SHRDLU is a landmark AI program written by Terry Winograd in 1968-1971, at MIT. Though the symbolic approach to language understanding has fallen out of favor since then, few projects since have achieved anything like the language facility of SHRDLU.

Originally written for MACLISP, a long-dead dialect of Lisp, for MIT's Incompatible Time Sharing system (ITS), a long-dead operating system, it was ported to Common Lisp (CLisp to be precise) in the 1990s by a group of students in Missouri. Tom Sgouros, a programmer at Brown University, has fixed a few bugs from that port, written a web-compatible graphics front end, and equipped SHRDLU with new digs here on the internet.

Close this window and click the "S" icon below to get started. Type your commands in the form of English sentences, and end them with a period, question mark or exclamation point. In the process of being ported twice, SHRDLU has lost some of its original functionality. Think of this version as SHRDLU that has suffered a minor stroke. Despite that limitation, SHRDLU can still accommodate requests like "find a block smaller than the one you are holding and put it in the box." But don't take my word for it; give it a try.
